What It's Called What it does/Why you need it Comments
Equipment
PCR Machine/Thermal Cycler Using controlled temperature cycling, causes PCR reactions to occur.  
Pipettors Measures very tiny amounts of liquids precisely Multiple sizes will be needed. We sugest your lab acquires P2, P20, P200, & P1000 models. The number signifies the maximum amount of liquid the pipettor will draw in µliters.
Centrifuge Spins tubes very fast to separate materials of different densities Start with at least a microfuge (spins 1.5mL tubes) and get your hands on a larger refrigerated 'fuge if you can later to spin 15mL and 50mL tubes.
Gel Electrophoresis Box Device that uses electric current to separate DNA fragments based on size.  
Gel Electrophoresis Power Supply Used to power an electrophoresis box Should supply a voltage up to 200 volts
Cold storage devices
Refrigerator   A small 4.4 cf ft refrigerator will suffice
Constant Temperature Freezer (-20°C), manual defrost model   Most modern freezers have a freeze/thaw cycle that keeps frost from building up. For the lab, the freezer must not have such a cycle and must maintain a constant termperature.
Liquid Nitrogen or -80C freezer For long-term sample storage and cells such as competent E. coli Usually on the wish list but too expensive to buy immediately
Incubator (37°C) Maintains a constant temperature to grow cultures  
Vortex Mixer Used to quickly mix liquids in a tube by vibrating rapidly  
Balances Analytical balance (mg weights) and top-loader (grams) You will eventaully need this. Try to get the top-loaded immediately and then hunt for the analytical.
UV or Blue Light Box Used to visualize stained DNA in electrophoresis gels If you use Ethidium Bromide, you need a UV box -include a plexi shield so as not to damage your retinas! Most DIYbio groups have switched to SybrGreen, a non-hazardous stain that can be visualized in blue light or UV light.
Microwave Oven   Small size required. Should be large enough to hold a 250 ml erlenmeyer flask. For research use ONLY. If you want to make popcorn or heat up lunch get an additional oven.
Glassware    
Erlenmeyer Flasks   250 ml size
Graduated Cylinders   100 ml and 1000 ml size
Beakers   200 ml
Autoclave or pressure cooker steam sterilizer, used to make sterile media, sterilize objects very expensive- the pressure cooker is the poor man's alternative but a real autoclave is better. If you can find one on eBay and can afford it, get one!
water bath or heat block Maintains a constant temperature, can be used to heat tubes in experiments or thaw things  
shaker or rotator needed for bacterial culture a combination incubator-shaker is best, but pricey. You can put a small tube rotator in the 37C incubator to achieve the same effect. Your cultures will just grow a little slower.
