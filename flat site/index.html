What is an Open Lab and Why Start One?

Open labs (also called community labs or biohackerspaces) have reframed the biolab as a community resource. Serving the general public, their mission is to lower the barriers to access for anyone who wants to practice biotechnology or synthetic biology. In an Open Lab, the cost of research and personal exploration in the biosciences is lowered through the sharing of space and equipment. They are perfect spaces for informal science education, communication of science to the public, and innovation across disciplines. They are a relatively new phenomenon, with more being founded every year. Open Labs attract a diverse, vibrant community of makers and citizen scientists. We at Genspace want to share the experience of our community and help YOU begin the adventure of starting one in YOUR city!
